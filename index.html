Key Terminology

Frozen Pipe
A pipe that has been exposed to freezing temperatures, causing the water inside to freeze and potentially burst the pipe.
Thawing Process
The method used to restore a frozen pipe to its normal state by gradually warming it up to avoid further damage.
Hydro Jetting
A technique used to clear obstructions in pipes using high-pressure water, often used in clearing ice or debris from frozen pipes.
Slab Leak
A leak that occurs in pipes beneath the foundation of a building, which can become exacerbated during freezing conditions.
Pipe Insulation
The process of covering pipes with materials that prevent heat loss and reduce the risk of freezing during cold temperatures.

Process & Diagnostics

The process of repairing frozen pipes begins with a diagnostic evaluation, which identifies the location of the frozen pipes and assesses the extent of any damage. Tools like thermal cameras and electronic leak detection equipment are used to locate blockages and leaks. Once the frozen pipes are identified, the repair process involves thawing the pipes, repairing or replacing damaged sections, and applying insulation or other preventative measures to avoid future freezing.
